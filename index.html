Step 1a · Label Construction ⚠ Leakage Intentional
⚠ Labels are constructed as forward-looking rolling returns over a k-day window. This creates look-ahead bias: each training sample uses future price information, ensuring leakage between any train/test split.

Step 3 · Leakage Mitigation: Purged Walk-Forward

Introduce an embargo (purge) gap between training and test windows to prevent forward-looking overlap. Repeat the 500/500 and 500/100 experiments and observe the change in apparent performance.

Step 3a · Leakage Mitigation Method Purge + Embargo
ℹ Method: A purge gap (embargo period) of 10 observations is inserted between the training window and the test window. Any training samples whose label overlaps with the embargo period are removed from the training set. This prevents temporal leakage from forward-looking labels (horizon k = 5 days). The embargo length is set to at least k (label horizon) to guarantee zero overlap.
